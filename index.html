Netaji Subhash Chandra Bose

"Give me blood, and I shall give you freedom"

About

Subhash Chandra Bose, also known as Netaji, was one of the most prominent leaders in India's struggle for independence. Born on 23 January 1897 in Cuttack, Odisha, Bose was a nationalist whose defiant patriotism made him a hero in India. He played a key role in organizing the Indian National Army (INA) to fight against British colonial rule.

Timeline

  • 1897 - Born in Cuttack, Odisha.
  • 1920s - Joined Indian National Congress and became a youth leader.
  • 1938 - Elected President of Indian National Congress.
  • 1943 - Formed the Indian National Army (INA).
  • 1945 - Reported to have died in a plane crash (mystery still surrounds his death).

Achievements & Honours

  • Founded the Azad Hind Fauj (INA) to fight for India’s independence.
  • Established the Azad Hind Government in exile in 1943.
  • Inspired millions with his slogan “Jai Hind”.
  • Recognized as a national hero in India and abroad.
  • Many institutions, airports, and universities are named after him, including Netaji Subhash Chandra Bose International Airport, Kolkata.

Famous Quote

"Freedom is not given, it is taken."
